# Knowledge Recovery Engine

Version: 1.0

Status: Core Innovation

---

# Purpose

The Adaptive Recall Engine determines what a student should revise and when.

Its goal is to maximize long-term retention by scheduling reviews before concepts are forgotten.

---

# Core Philosophy

Students should not decide what to revise.

EduPath decides using evidence.

---

# Inputs

The engine considers:

- Confidence Score
- Time Since Last Review
- Concept Difficulty
- Exam Importance
- Previous Mistakes
- Revision Count
- Learning Graph Dependencies

---

# Forgetting Curve

Each concept slowly loses confidence over time.

Confidence decreases faster for:

- difficult concepts
- infrequently revised topics
- repeatedly incorrect questions

Regular retrieval strengthens memory.

---

# Daily Brain Warm-Up

Every day EduPath generates:

5–10 personalized questions.

Question selection balances:

- Recent topics
- Older forgotten concepts
- Weak areas
- High-weight exam topics

---

# Priority Formula

Priority increases when:

- Confidence decreases.
- Last revision is old.
- Exam importance is high.
- The topic unlocks many future concepts.

---

# Revision Types

Quick Recall

Short concept checks.

---

Concept Recovery

Focused revision of forgotten material.

---

Mastery Reinforcement

Higher-difficulty questions.

---

Exam Readiness

Mixed previous-year questions.

---

# AI Explanation

Every recommendation must explain itself.

Example:

"This concept hasn't been revised for 10 days and supports three upcoming chapters."

---

# Success Criteria

Students spend less time deciding what to study and more time actively recalling knowledge.
