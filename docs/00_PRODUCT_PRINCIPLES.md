# Product Principles

> "Products become coherent when every feature follows the same philosophy."

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines the non-negotiable principles that guide every product and engineering decision in EduPath.

Whenever a new feature is proposed, it must align with these principles.

If it violates them, the feature should be redesigned or rejected.

---

# Principle 1

## Retention over Completion

EduPath does not measure success by the number of chapters completed.

It measures success by how much knowledge the student still remembers weeks later.

Every feature should improve long-term retention.

Never optimize solely for finishing content.

---

# Principle 2

## Retrieval Before Explanation

The brain remembers information better when it actively retrieves it.

Whenever appropriate:

Ask first.

Explain later.

Instead of immediately giving answers, encourage students to recall what they already know.

---

# Principle 3

## AI Should Remember

The AI should never behave like it has forgotten the student's learning journey.

It should remember:

- previous mistakes
- confidence levels
- learning preferences
- completed topics
- revision history
- recurring misconceptions

Persistence is one of EduPath's defining characteristics.

---

# Principle 4

## Every Interaction Should Strengthen Memory

Every interaction should improve one or more of:

- recall
- confidence
- understanding
- consistency
- motivation

Avoid interactions that create unnecessary cognitive load.

---

# Principle 5

## Personalization Over Generalization

No two students learn identically.

Recommendations should consider:

- exam goal
- confidence
- weak concepts
- available study time
- historical performance
- learning pace

---

# Principle 6

## Reward Learning, Not Usage

Do not reward:

- logging in
- clicking buttons
- spending time

Reward:

- recovering forgotten topics
- maintaining retention
- mastering difficult concepts
- consistency
- solving previous-year questions

---

# Principle 7

## Explain Every Recommendation

Students should understand why the AI is making recommendations.

Example:

"Your retention for Electrostatics has dropped to 58%, so we're reviewing it today."

Never make AI decisions feel random.

---

# Principle 8

## Confidence Matters

Knowing something and feeling confident about it are different.

Track both independently.

Confidence should influence:

- revision frequency
- question difficulty
- study plans

---

# Principle 9

## Build Confidence Gradually

Difficulty should increase progressively.

Concept

↓

Recall

↓

Application

↓

Previous-Year Questions

↓

Mixed Mock Tests

Students should experience measurable growth.

---

# Principle 10

## Make Progress Visible

Students should always know:

- what improved
- what weakened
- what to revise
- why they're improving

Learning should feel tangible.

---

# Principle 11

## Celebrate Recovery

Recovering forgotten knowledge is more valuable than learning something new.

EduPath should celebrate recovered memories.

Not just completed lessons.

---

# Principle 12

## The Student Is Never Punished

Mistakes are signals.

Not failures.

Incorrect answers should generate:

- better explanations
- adaptive revision
- encouragement

Never shame the learner.

---

# Principle 13

## AI Should Feel Like A Mentor

The AI should be:

- encouraging
- patient
- proactive
- insightful

Never robotic.

Never passive.

---

# Principle 14

## Reduce Decision Fatigue

Students already make hundreds of decisions.

EduPath should reduce unnecessary choices by recommending:

- today's revision
- today's study plan
- next best concept

---

# Principle 15

## Build for Trust

The AI should never invent progress.

Never fake understanding.

Never hide uncertainty.

Transparency builds confidence.

---

# Final Principle

Every feature should answer one question.

"Will this genuinely help a student remember better?"

If the answer is no,

the feature probably doesn't belong in EduPath.
