# Product Lexicon

Version: 1.0

Status: Brand Identity

---

# Purpose

This document defines the official terminology used throughout EduPath.

The goal is to create a consistent product language that is memorable for users, intuitive for developers, and recognizable during demonstrations.

All future UI, documentation, prompts, and marketing material should follow this lexicon.

# Core Systems

## Cognitive Learning Engine (CLE)

The central intelligence system responsible for understanding, predicting, and improving student learning.

---

## Memory Engine

Maintains persistent knowledge about each student's learning history, strengths, weaknesses, and preferences.

---

## Knowledge Constellation

The user-facing visualization of the Learning Graph.

Represents every concept as an interconnected network.

Concept mastery illuminates the constellation.

Knowledge decay causes stars to dim.

---

## Educational Reasoning Engine (ERE)

The reasoning component inside the CLE.

Determines why recommendations should be made.

Predicts future learning outcomes.

---

## Educational Reasoning Pipeline (ERP)

The internal reasoning workflow used by the ERE.

Observation

↓

Interpretation

↓

Dependency Analysis

↓

Prediction

↓

Planning

↓

Reflection

---

## Knowledge Recovery Engine

Determines what concepts should be recovered and when.

Schedules personalized retrieval practice.

---

## Recommendation Engine

Produces personalized learning plans based on outputs from all CLE components.

---

# Learning Metrics

## Retention Index

Overall long-term learning health.

Displayed as a percentage.

Primary success metric.

---

## Memory Drift

Measures how quickly a student's knowledge is expected to decay.

Higher Drift = Higher revision priority.

---

## Confidence Score

Confidence for an individual concept.

Updated after quizzes and study sessions.

---

## Mastery Level

Represents depth of understanding.

Examples:

Beginner

Developing

Proficient

Mastered

---

## Learning Pulse

Overall learning momentum.

Represents consistency and recent progress.

---

## Why Today?

## Learning Path

# Naming Principles

All terminology should:

• Be educational rather than game-like.

• Encourage growth instead of competition.

• Be understandable to students globally.

• Remain technically meaningful for developers.

• Reflect the philosophy of long-term learning rather than short-term performance.
